HCI + Design + Computing Education.

I'm a PhD student in the Human-Centered Design and Engineering Department at the University of Washington, currently working with Prof. Julie Kientz in the Computing for Healthy Living and Learning Lab. My research interest is in HCI in family-centered technology and healthcare. I am particularly interested in participatory design methods for more diverse and inclusive designs

Prior to attending UW, I was a Project Manager and Design Researcher at the Engagement Lab at Emerson College, working on civic media technology and research. In 2018, I managed the Make the Breast Pump Not Suck Hackathon. at the MIT Media Lab, to foster interdisciplinary collaboration for equity-driven postpartum healthcare in the United States. In my free time, I am learning to take care of a garden and enjoying hiking in the Pacific Northwest.

I am currently seeking design-research internship or contract opportunities for the summer of 2020.
